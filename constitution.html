Constitutional Summary

Preamble

We, the people of Meridionalis, in order to establish justice, ensure domestic tranquility, provide for the common defense, promote the general welfare, and secure the blessings of liberty to ourselves and our posterity, do ordain and establish this Constitution for the Federal Republic of Meridionalis.

Article I: Fundamental Rights

  • Freedom of speech, press, religion, assembly, and petition
  • Right to bear arms for self-defense and national defense
  • Protection against unreasonable searches and seizures
  • Right to due process and equal protection under the law
  • Prohibition of cruel and unusual punishment

Article II: Structure of Government

The government is divided into three branches:

  1. Legislative: Bicameral Parliament (Senate and National Assembly)
  2. Executive: President and Vice President with Cabinet
  3. Judicial: Supreme Court and lower courts

Article III: Federal System

Meridionalis is a federal republic composed of 12 states and 3 federal territories. Powers are divided between the federal government and state governments.

Article IV: Amendment Process

The Constitution may be amended by:

  1. Two-thirds vote in both houses of Parliament, followed by ratification by three-fourths of state legislatures
  2. Constitutional Convention called by two-thirds of state legislatures

Constitutional History

1854

Original Constitution Adopted

The Constitution was drafted by the Founding Assembly and ratified by all 8 original states, establishing the Federal Republic of Meridionalis.

1872

First Amendments (1-10)

The Bill of Rights was added, guaranteeing fundamental freedoms and protections for citizens.

1910

Women's Suffrage Amendment

Amendment 15 granted women the right to vote in all federal and state elections.

1948

Post-War Reforms

Amendments 22-25 restructured parts of government and added social welfare provisions following the Great War.

1992

Modernization Amendments

Amendments 26-27 updated provisions for the digital age and clarified presidential succession.

2021

Latest Amendment

Amendment 28 established term limits for members of Parliament.

Constitutional Amendments

Amendment I (1872)

Freedom of religion, speech, press, assembly, and petition.

Amendment II (1872)

Right to keep and bear arms for self-defense and national defense.

Amendment XV (1910)

Women's suffrage - the right of citizens to vote shall not be denied on account of sex.

Amendment XXII (1948)

Limits presidential terms to two four-year terms.

Amendment XXVI (1992)

Establishes privacy rights in the digital age.

Amendment XXVIII (2021)

Limits members of Parliament to four consecutive terms in the same house.
