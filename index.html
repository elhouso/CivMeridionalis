Government Structure

Elections

National elections are held every 6 months to select the President and Vice President. All citizens in good standing are eligible to vote.

The election process consists of:

  1. Nomination period (1 week)
  2. Campaign period (2 weeks)
  3. Voting day (24 hours)
  4. Results announcement

Voting System

We use a ranked-choice voting system where citizens can rank candidates in order of preference. This ensures the winning candidate has majority support.

Legislative Process

New laws can be proposed by any citizen. The process is as follows:

  1. Draft proposal submitted
  2. Public discussion period (1 week)
  3. Amendment phase
  4. Final vote by all citizens
  5. Presidential approval

Executive Branch

The President is responsible for:

  • Implementing laws
  • Foreign relations
  • Military command
  • Appointing ministers
